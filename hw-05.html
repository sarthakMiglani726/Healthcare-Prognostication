# Extracting the relevant columns for age groups and dementia risk
age_group_columns = [col for col in data1.columns if col.startswith('age group:')]
age_group_data = data1[age_group_columns]
dementia_risk = data1['predicted risk of Dementia']

# Preparing the data for the plot
# Summing up the number of people in each age group
age_group_totals = age_group_data.sum()

# Calculating the average risk of dementia for each age group
average_risk_per_age_group = {}
for col in age_group_columns:
    # Getting the indices where the age group is 1 (i.e., the patient belongs to that age group)
    indices = age_group_data[age_group_data[col] == 1].index
    # Calculating the average risk for this age group
    average_risk_per_age_group[col] = dementia_risk[indices].mean()

# Converting to a format suitable for plotting
age_groups = list(average_risk_per_age_group.keys())
average_risks = list(average_risk_per_age_group.values())

# Creating a horizontal bar plot for average risk of dementia by age group

plt.figure(figsize=(12, 8))

# Plotting horizontal bars
plt.barh(age_groups, average_risks, color='darkorange')

# Adding labels to each bar for clarity
for index, value in enumerate(average_risks):
    plt.text(value, index, f'{value:.4f}', va='center', ha='left', fontsize=8)

# Enhancing the overall graph aesthetics
plt.ylabel('Age Group', fontsize=14, labelpad=15)

plt.yticks(fontsize=12)

plt.grid(axis='x', linestyle='--', alpha=0.7)

The horizontal bar plot visualizes the average risk of dementia for various age groups. Each bar length represents the magnitude of risk, with annotated values offering precise risk percentages. The data has been transformed and now includes pivoted columns for age groups and sex. Here's a summary of the modifications:

  • Column Renaming: All column names were reformatted to lowercase and spaces were replaced with underscores.
  • Pivoting Age Group Columns: The age group data, previously spread across multiple columns, has been consolidated into two columns: age_group and age_count. The age_group column specifies the age group, and age_count indicates the presence (1) or absence (0) of a patient in that age group.
  • Pivoting Sex Columns: Similar to age groups, sex data has also been consolidated into two columns: sex and sex_count. The sex column specifies the sex, and sex_count indicates the presence (1) or absence (0) of a patient in that sex category.
  • Cleaning Column Values: The values in age_group and sex columns were cleaned to remove the preceding text ('age_group_' and 'sex_').

Assumption Checks¶

  • Linearity Check (Observed vs. Predicted): The scatter plot does not show a perfect linear relationship, indicating potential non-linearity or the need for different/additional variables to capture a linear relationship.

  • Independence Check (Durbin-Watson Test): The Durbin-Watson statistic is 2.16, which is close to 2, suggesting that the residuals are independent.

  • Homoscedasticity Check (Residuals vs. Predicted values): The scatter plot of residuals versus predicted values displays some pattern, indicating that homoscedasticity may not hold.

  • Normality of Residuals (Q-Q Plot): The Q-Q plot shows significant deviation from the line, particularly at the tails, suggesting that the residuals are not normally distributed. This is confirmed by the Shapiro-Wilk test, which returned a p-value of 0.0, indicating non-normality of residuals.
